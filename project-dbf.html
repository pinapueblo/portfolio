Project — RC aircraft

Texas Design Build Fly RC Plane

As the Composites Engineer for UT Austin's Design Build Fly team, I designed and manufactured lightweight composite fuselage tooling and carbon fiber structures for an AIAA competition aircraft. My work focused on transforming aerodynamic CAD models into manufacturable composite components using custom 3D-printed molds, hybrid carbon fiber/Kevlar layups, and vacuum bagging while meeting strict weight and structural requirements.

Engineering Highlights

Project Objective

  • Design and manufacture a lightweight composite fuselage and landing gear for AIAA Design/Build/Fly.
  • Meet strict weight, aerodynamic efficiency, and structural stiffness requirements.
  • Produce a competition-ready airframe capable of carrying heavy payloads.

My Role

  • Designed composite tooling in SOLIDWORKS from concept through production.
  • Developed carbon fiber/Kevlar layup schedules and executed vacuum bagging.
  • Collaborated with the aerodynamics team to convert CAD surfaces into manufacturable structures.

Manufacturing

  • Designed two negative molds split into eight printable PETG sections.
  • Bonded molds with epoxy reinforced using CAB-O-SIL before sanding and polishing to a mirror finish.
  • Produced four composite fuselage iterations to optimize stiffness-to-weight ratio.
  • Engineered landing gear using ±45° carbon fiber and 0° Kevlar reinforcement.

Results

  • 6th place worldwide among 100+ teams at the 2025 AIAA Design/Build/Fly competition.
  • Validated an 8G airframe carrying a 70 lb payload with no visible fuselage deformation.
  • Achieved zero delamination during structural testing and maintained high dimensional accuracy.

Vacuum bag process

The vacuum bagging stage was one of the most important manufacturing steps for achieving a smooth, low-weight composite shell.

  • Layup schedule tuned for strength-to-weight efficiency.
  • Bagging setup used to consolidate the laminate and remove trapped air.
  • Critical for preserving surface quality and dimensional accuracy.

Weight testing

Structural validation included weight-focused tests to confirm the airframe stayed within competition requirements while maintaining stiffness.
